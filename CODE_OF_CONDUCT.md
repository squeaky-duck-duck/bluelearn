**Basic Member, Admin, and Moderator responsibilities & Code-Of-Conduct**
# MEMBERS

The Members of the B.L.U.E. System project are the community which hold the project up, and form it's base.  A strong base creates a strong foundation for the project, and by that same token, a weak base creates a weak foundation.  As such, Members will be expected to meet certain standards.  The purpose of this part of the document is to outline those standards, and present the community code of conduct.

# COMMUNITY CODE-OF-CONDUCT

1. **Welcome & Purpose**
This server exists to foster a positive, welcoming, and productive community. By participating here, you agree to uphold these guidelines and contribute to a positive environment for everyone.

2. **Core Principles**
Be Respectful. Treat all members with courtesy and respect. Disagreements are natural, but express them constructively. Personal attacks, insults, and inflammatory behavior are not tolerated.

Be Helpful. Support fellow community members when you can. Share knowledge, answer questions, and elevate the discussion.

3. **Prohibited Conduct**
The following behaviors will result in moderation action:

- Harassment:	targeted insults, threats, doxxing, stalking, unwanted DM solicitation
- Spam: excessive self-promotion, repetitive messages, bot-like behavior, unsolicited invites, NSFW Content, - Sexual content, graphic violence, illegal activity
- Trolling: Intentional disruption, sealioning, bad-faith arguments, misdirection, and false claims about the B.L.U.E. project
- Impersonation: Pretending to be staff, pretending to be other members, or impersonating public figures

4. **Content Guidelines**
- Keep conversations about particular subjects to their respective forums and channels.  (E.g. Discussions about the Finances of B.L.U.E. should be kept in the #finance-general-chat or #finance-forum.)
- Stay on topic in each channel, and check channel descriptions before posting
- Credit creators when sharing art, code, or other work
- English in public channels so moderation can function
- No external drama—don't import conflicts from other communities

5. **Voice Chat Rules**
- Push-to-talk preferred in busy channels
- No voice changers, soundboards, or intentionally disruptive/obnoxious sounds
- No recording without all participants' permission
- No channel hopping or spam joining/leaving

6. **Moderation & Enforcement**
Violations are handled at staff discretion using a tiered approach:

Verbal Warning — Minor first-time offenses
Mute — Temporary restriction from chatting (hours to days)
Ban — Permanent removal
Serious violations (doxxing, threats, CSAM) result in immediate ban and may be reported to Discord Trust & Safety.

7. **Appeals & Reporting**
To report: DM a moderator or use the designated report channel/bot
To appeal: Contact staff via DM or the appeal form after 7 days
Evidence: Screenshots with message links help investigations

8. **Staff Discretion**
Moderators have final say in enforcement. These guidelines cannot cover every situation, and staff may take action against behavior that undermines community health even if not explicitly listed here.

# ADMINS AND MODERATORS:

The **Admins** and **Moderators** of the B.L.U.E. System project are the primary individuals who will be responsible for driving the overall direction of the project.  As such, they will have certain responsibilities and must meet certain standards to achieve the goals of the project effectively.  The purpose of this part of the document is to outline those basic standards and responsibilities, to eliminate confusion, and to give leads a clear idea of their role within the project.


## **Admins** 
- The highest role within the B.LU.E. System project, responsible for managing moderators and completing broad tasks, within their assigned sector of the project.

### **Admins MUST**
1. Be able and willing to act as a team

2. Understand the core values and current goals of the B.L.U.E. System Project, outlined by the project managers, and must act in accordance with those values, and goals.

3. Admins will be assigned to a particular area of areas of the B.L.U.E. project, must know the general tasks which currently need completion within those areas, and must be able to move effectively and efficiently toward.

4. Admins must be present to complete tasks in a timely manner.  Admins who have prolonged inactivity without providing reason, or show an inability to complete tasks, will be subject to peer review and possible demotion.

5. Admins must be able to understand GENERAL problems, solutions, and sentiments within sectors, and must be responsive to those problems, solutions, and sentiments.

6. Admins must also have enough skill/experience to understand problems at a TECHNICAL level, in order to make more specific determinations.

7. Admins must show sound judgment.  Admins who are (or who become) unfair, unreasonable, or unresponsive will be subject to peer review and possible demotion.

8. Admins must be able to handle a potentially heavy workload.

9. Admins must be able to understand and judge the significance of problems, and must be able to tell the difference between high level (more significant) problems and lower level (less significant) problems.

10. Admins should be spending most of their time designing/optimizing BLUE's structure & harnassing the talent within their assigned domains.

11. Admins should delegate lower level tasks to moderators/contributors and prioritize high level management 

12. Admins must be able to effectively manage Moderators.  When Moderators come to Admins with a problem or issue, Admins must be able to understand those issues, assess their significance, and generate swift & effective solutions.

13. Admins should be able to identify talent within their assigned domains, delegate tasks according, and offer promotion opportunities for those with special skill/talent/ability/contribution.

14. For high-level problems or decisions, **ADMINS MUST CONSULT OTHER ADMINS**.  Admins should not be making important decisions alone, except in the rare case where it's unavoidable to do so.  Admins who consistently act alone will be subject to peer review and possible demotion/removal.

15. **ADMINS MUST NOT UNDERMINE THE ACTIONS OF OTHER ADMINS.** If certain admins have agreed on an action or plan, other admins should NOT, under any circumstances, alter/undermine/subvert/or counter those actions & plans. The admins of the B.L.U.E. project are a team. If the team can't work together, the project will assuredly fall apart.

16. If an Admin has a serious concern about any aspect of the project, they must be willing to voice their concern or concerns.  The project can't improve without useful feedback.

17.  Admins must be 18 years or older.**(For NPO filling only)**.


**What Admins are NOT responsible for:**
- Low level tasks, as well as tasks & actions mainly meant for Moderators/contributors (e.g. Muting/Timing Out/Kicking/Banning chatters).

- **On Discord** Engaging with General/Community Chat.

- Organizing all aspects of the B.L.U.E. System project.

- Contributing to all domains/sectors.

- Solving technical problems in unassigned sectors.


### **Admin Suitability Assessment**

When assessing the suitability of an applicant for the role of Admin, the following factors must be considered:
- **Ability & willingness to work as team, and meet the above conditions.**

- **Temperament and Character** (individuals who consistently make emotionally-charged decisions, or who can't moderate their own behavior, should not be an Admin).

- **Area of Speciality** (what area, if any, does this individual specialize in?).

- **Actual Skill Level & Effectiveness** (how skilled are they in that area/areas?).

- **Ability to Generalize** (how well can this indivual understand and deal with GENERAL problems within their particular area).

- **"Sentiment Sense"** (does this individual have a sense of the "**zeitgest**" within a particular area?  In other words, how well do they understand the general sentiment/culture/feelings/"vibe" of an area?).

- **Sensitivity to feedback** (how responsive is this individual to feedback & changing conditions).


## **Moderators**
- The next highest role within the B.LU.E. System project, responsible for managing contributors and completing more specific tasks, within their assigned sector of the project.

### **Moderators MUST:**
1. be able and willing to act as a team, and must regularly communicate with other moderators and admins, to coordinate actions.

2. understand the core values and current goals of the B.L.U.E. System Project, outlined by the project managers, and must act in accordance with those values, and goals.

3. Moderators will be assigned to a particular area of the B.L.U.E. project, must know the tasks which currently need completion within those areas (given to them by Admins), and must be able to move effectively and efficiently toward.

4. Moderators must be present to complete tasks in a timely manner.  Moderators who have prolonged inactivity without providing reason, or show an inability to complete tasks, will be subject to peer review and possible demotion.

5. Moderators must be able to understand SPECIFIC problems within their sector/task area, and must have the technical skill/experience to handle those problems.

6. Moderators must show sound judgment.  Mods who are (or who become) unfair, unreasonable, or unresponsive will be subject to peer review and possible demotion.

7. Moderators must be able to handle a potentially heavy workload, depending on their area.

8. Moderators must be able to understand and judge the significance of problems, and must be able to tell the difference between high level (more significant) problems and lower level (less significant) problems.

9. Problems which have no clear solution, or which involve a higher level issue, should be explained/passed up to Admins.

10. Moderators must be able to effectively manage lower-level contributions/commenters.  When commenters/contributors come to Mods with a problem or issue, Mods must be able to understand those issues, assess their significance, and generate swift & effective solutions.

11. Moderators should be able to spot talent, and make suggestions about the promotion of lower level contributors/members.

12. **MODERATORS MUST NOT UNDERMINE THE ACTIONS OF OTHER MODERATORS/ADMINS**.  If certain moderators or admins have agreed on an action or plan, other moderators should NOT, under any circumstances, alter/undermine/subvert/or counter those actions & plans.

13. If a Moderator has a serious concern about any aspect of the project, they must be willing to voice their concern or concerns to Admins.  The project can't improve without useful feedback.

14. **On Discord** Moderators must be reasonable and professional.  Moderators should not be getting into long, drawn out engagements with disrespectful commenters.  Instead, they must give simple, brief warnings. (1 or 2 warnings maximum).  If warnings are ignored, commenter should be muted, If commenter continues with behavior after muting, timeout should be implemented.  If commenter continues behavior after timeout, ban should be implemented after discussion with other **moderators** and **admins**.

15. Moderator shouldn't ping admins for every problem. Proper discussions should be done before pinging anyone. if it's related to moderation then let **@beastrg(Benzo)** or **@hadie20002(Hadie202)** know.

What Moderators are **NOT** responsible for:
- **Higher level tasks** (including project organization & structuring), as well as tasks & actions mainly meant for lower level contributors.
- **Contributing to all domains/sectors**.
- **Solving problems in unassigned sectors**.
  

### **Moderator Suitability Assessment**

When assessing the suitability of an applicant for the role of Moderator, the following factors must be considered:
- **Ability & willingness to work as team, and meet the above conditions.**
- **Temperament and Character** (individuals who consistently make emotionally-charged decisions, or who can't moderate their own behavior, should not be a Moderator).
- **Area of Speciality** (what area, if any, does this individual specialize in?).
- **Actual Skill Level & Effectiveness** (how skilled are they in that area/areas?).
- **Sensitivity to feedback** (how responsive is this individual to feedback & changing conditions).


## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — the GitHub
organization, issues, pull requests, the Discord server, project events,
and any time someone is representing Bluelearn in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates BLUE's Code Of Conduct, please
report it privately to:

**conduct@bluelearn.org**

Reports are received by the project leads and are kept confidential.
Reporters will not be retaliated against.

We will respond within 7 days with next steps. Enforcement decisions
follow the Code of Conduct's enforcement ladder (correction →
warning → temporary ban → permanent ban) at the discretion of the
project leads.
